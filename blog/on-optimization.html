On Optimization

2026-03-24

WIP

What do I mean by optimization? Optimization to me is something as follows: Choosing the right steps to reach an optimal outcome. More specifcally, I want to sort of talk about my thoughts on Optimization of your college experience.

So what is optimizing your college experience?

I think it's hard to explain without some examples. One person I met during the first couple weeks at college told me "I want to go into investment banking!". At the time, I did not know what investment banking was. I only knew that they were applying to all of these finance clubs and networking with upperclassmen. In their second year at the university, they started to take accounting courses and these business electives. One quarter, I barely saw them at all. They were doing applications on banking internships for the next summer, and that to me shocked me. Many people I knew who were going down this route were doing the exact same, and this was considered the "optimal" approach. I was shocked to even know that these people even knew that they wanted to go down this route. This kind of joining these clubs, meeting people who can guide you through these processes, and other stuff is an example of optimizing your career path. Quants may join the local quantitative trading club or vice versa. Pre-meds may join a local bio lab.

But even optimization itself can be applied to other places. If you knew that you wanted to join a good social life, maybe you would have joined greek life. Maybe you joined a professional organization and really hit it off with people. Optimization can take many different forms.

But how do you know what to optimize for? What do I have to do?

Short answer: You will only know what to optimize for once you have information. And even then, that information has to be information that is deemed "optimal". How do you know what to do? Do some research. Talk to upperclassmen. Read into what you are interested in and see what worked for people. You can formulate a gameplan from there.

But is there a truely optimal path?

I truely think that there does not exist an optimal path. If there was an optimal path and there was a way to reach it, everyone would do it. But, it is a competitive world and there are many people who take what they deem as the optimal path and do not succeed. I think that optimal paths are local in a sense. Sure, there are some things that you should do objectively to get far in what you want to do, i.e networking for bankers. But, even then those are sometimes not necessary. You yourself are different from others, and that path doesn't have to be what other people do. You have to do what works for you. Take the optimal path for what information you have at the time.

Should I always take the optimal path

No. There is more to life than just blindly doing the things that will strictly further your career. I think I can illustrate this best using a personal andecote. A friend once asked me "If you are so set on this goal, why aren't you doing 30 hours a week on doing this goal?"Answer: I don't. It is because I don't think that's how I want to live my life. My life is a lot more than just doing problems and interview prepping. Sure, these sort of things are indeed important and I should (and am) dedicating time to these things, but I have other things I find important in life and bring me joy. Maybe other people think that brings happiness, but for me, that is not the case.
